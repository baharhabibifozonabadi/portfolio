Computational Collaboration: Persian OCR and Literary Analysis

Click to expand

This project was conducted as part of the master's thesis of Hossein Babaei, supervised by the Department of Literature at the University of Tehran, and represents an interdisciplinary collaboration between computational methods and Persian literary scholarship. The overarching goal was to develop a system capable of reading and analyzing Persian manuscripts in the classical Nastaʿlīq script, a script known for its intricate ligatures, calligraphic flourishes, and historical variations, which pose considerable challenges for textual transcription.

My role in the project involved developing recognition techniques, curating textual corpora, and designing methodological protocols. The project began with a corpus of modern Persian texts to establish baseline transcription accuracy, and progressively included classical and historical manuscripts to address stylistic complexity and variations across time periods.

In parallel, I collaborated closely with professors of Persian literature and manuscript studies, contributing to the development of diagnostic and analytical criteria for textual validation and literary analysis. These criteria enabled the system to not only produce accurate transcriptions but also compare multiple versions of the same historical work, identify discrepancies, and support the reconstruction of original manuscripts—a process that traditionally requires years of expert scholarly work.

This work demonstrates a synergistic approach between computational techniques, literary scholarship, and manuscript studies. By integrating technical methods with humanities research, it provides a platform for the efficient study and preservation of Persian literary heritage. My contributions highlight active engagement in both technical implementation and literary-methodological design, reflecting the interdisciplinary nature of the project and its significance for advancing digital humanities research.

Key Responsibilities and Workflow:

  • Curated and structured textual corpora, from modern Persian texts to classical manuscripts.
  • Developed transcription and recognition techniques for Nastaʿlīq manuscripts.
  • Collaborated with faculty to define textual validation and literary analysis criteria.
  • Designed protocols for comparing multiple manuscript versions and reconstructing originals.
  • Coordinated interdisciplinary efforts, integrating computational methods with Persian literary scholarship.

Transcription and Analysis of Therapy Sessions for Digital Patient Records

Click to expand

This project focuses on the systematic recording, transcription, and analysis of therapy session audio, aimed at producing structured digital files that capture both the verbal and interactional dynamics of therapeutic encounters. While the technical implementation of the audio recording and transcription software was handled by other team members, my role centered on the analytical and methodological aspects of the project, bridging applied psychology principles with structured data analysis.

Specifically, my work involves analyzing the transcribed sessions using established note-taking and behavioral observation frameworks, such as the Gottman Method, Reflective Practice Notes, and APA Behavioral Coding standards. These methods provide a structured approach to capturing nuanced interpersonal dynamics, emotional exchanges, and behavioral patterns in therapy, allowing for both qualitative and quantitative insights. By combining these frameworks with speech and conversation analysis techniques, particularly Conversation Analysis (CA), I am able to examine turn-taking, interruptions, overlaps, speech repair strategies, and other features of spoken interaction that reveal underlying psychological processes and relational patterns.

In practice, my workflow includes carefully reviewing each transcribed session, annotating behavioral and linguistic cues, and categorizing them according to the relevant theoretical frameworks. For example, the Gottman Method helps identify relational dynamics and communication patterns within couples or group therapy, while Reflective Practice Notes allow for the therapist's subjective insights and interpretations to be systematically incorporated into the dataset. APA Behavioral Coding provides standardized categories for emotional expressions, coping mechanisms, and client engagement, enabling cross-session and cross-client comparison. Conversation Analysis then allows for detailed micro-level examination of interactional sequences, revealing patterns that might be invisible in narrative or thematic analysis alone.

The culmination of this work is a comprehensive, structured digital file for each therapy session, synthesizing multiple layers of analysis—verbal content, behavioral coding, interactional patterns, and reflective notes. These files are designed not only for therapeutic documentation and clinical review, but also for research purposes, providing a rich dataset for analyzing psychological and relational dynamics across clients and sessions. The output is both scalable and standardized, ensuring consistent application of analytical frameworks across different therapists, sessions, and therapeutic modalities.

Importantly, this product is commercially oriented and has been integrated into applied digital health services. We are collaborating with platforms such as دکترتو and اسنپ دکتر, enabling the structured session analysis to be offered as part of their suite of therapeutic services. This collaboration ensures that therapists using these platforms can benefit from high-quality session documentation, objective behavioral insights, and evidence-informed feedback to improve clinical outcomes.

Overall, this project represents a sophisticated intersection of applied psychology, structured behavioral analysis, and digital service delivery, where my contributions focus on methodological rigor, analysis design, and practical implementation within real-world therapeutic contexts. It demonstrates how analytical frameworks from psychology can be operationalized into actionable digital outputs, providing meaningful support for therapists, clients, and healthcare platforms alike.

Key Responsibilities and Workflow:

  • Reviewed and analyzed transcribed therapy sessions using Gottman Method, Reflective Practice Notes, and APA Behavioral Coding.
  • Applied Conversation Analysis (CA) to examine micro-level interactional dynamics in therapy sessions.
  • Integrated multiple layers of analysis—verbal, behavioral, interactional, and reflective—into a comprehensive digital record.
  • Developed standardized protocols for documenting, coding, and summarizing sessions to ensure consistency and reliability.
  • Collaborated with faculty and clinical experts to refine analytical frameworks and ensure evidence-based application.
  • Coordinated with commercial platforms such as دکترتو and اسنپ دکتر to deploy the structured session analysis as part of their therapeutic service offerings.
  • Contributed to the scalable design of a digital tool that bridges applied psychology research with practical, real-world therapy documentation.

Collaboration in Applied Psychology: pesiko Startup

Click to expand

This project, conducted during 1399–1400, involved active collaboration with the pesiko psychology startup, with the goal of developing a comprehensive digital mental health education platform. The primary objective was to create a series of educational texts and videos, curated and structured within an algorithmic framework designed to deliver personalized learning experiences to users based on their individual needs and psychological profiles.

My role in the project was content creation and media production, working closely under the supervision of a licensed psychologist. I authored educational texts and produced accompanying videos that translated complex psychological concepts into accessible, engaging formats suitable for a broad audience. These materials were designed to align with evidence-based psychological knowledge and pedagogical best practices, ensuring both accuracy and usability.

The project was structured to complement monthly doctor appointments and therapy sessions, integrating digital learning with real-world therapeutic guidance. The algorithm was intended to adapt dynamically to the user's progress and needs, providing targeted content for self-guided learning, skill development, and mental health support.

Despite careful planning and production, the project encountered technical and organizational challenges, resulting in a halt before public launch. Nonetheless, the experience allowed me to combine applied psychological theory with digital content design, learning how to translate research-informed knowledge into engaging, multimedia educational products.

Key Responsibilities and Workflow:

  • Authored educational texts on psychological topics, designed for accessibility and engagement.
  • Produced accompanying educational videos under psychologist supervision.
  • Assisted in conceptualizing the algorithmic curation of content for personalized user experiences.
  • Coordinated project planning with clinical staff and development teams to align content with therapeutic objectives.
  • Contributed to a proof-of-concept platform integrating digital learning and mental health support.

This project exemplifies my ability to translate theoretical psychological knowledge into practical, educational, and digitally delivered content, reflecting the interdisciplinary intersection of psychology, media production, and e-learning design.
