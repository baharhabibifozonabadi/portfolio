**Title for Portfolio:**
*“Ideal: A Bunraku Reinterpretation of Mirzadeh Eshghi’s Verse Drama”*

**Short Description for Portfolio:**
This project combines classical Persian verse drama with Japanese Bunraku puppetry, reimagining *Ideal* to explore the Constitutional Revolution and the clash of tradition and modernity. Puppets, stage design, and music create a multisensory, interdisciplinary experience, while the narrator bridges the audience and story, enhancing the poetic text’s impact.

Title for Portfolio:
“Contemporary Iranian Playwrights: Saedi, Nalbandian, Yaghubi, and Rezaeirad”

Short Description for Portfolio:
This study highlights the diverse approaches of modern Iranian playwrights. Saedi combines leftist political critique with socially aware narratives; Nalbandian explores metaphysical and fragmented storytelling; Yaghubi presents human relationships and universal social issues through straightforward, episodic tales; Rezaeirad blends research-driven form with symbolic and historical motifs. Together, their works showcase interdisciplinary intersections of literature, society, and performative art.

Title for Portfolio:
“Nirsadat and Atri Sadat: Two Sisters, One Legend”

Short Description for Portfolio:
Based on research with middle-aged Iranian women, this project examines diverse perceptions of common Persian folktales. Presented as a narrative with two contrasting symbolic characters, it retells Iranian legends while highlighting the multiplicity of interpretation and cultural memory.
